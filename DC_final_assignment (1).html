Data Transmission Techniques

Simplex

→

One-way communication only

Example: Radio broadcasting, TV broadcasting.

Advantage: Simple and cost-effective.

Disadvantage: No feedback or acknowledgment from receiver.

Half-Duplex

↔

Two-way communication, but only one at a time

Example: Walkie-talkies, Two-way radio communication.

Advantage: Efficient use of bandwidth as both devices can communicate.

Disadvantage: Only one device can transmit at a time, leading to potential delays.

Full-Duplex

→
←

Simultaneous two-way communication

Example: Telephone networks, Fiber optic communication.

Advantage: Simultaneous two-way communication increases efficiency.

Disadvantage: More complex and expensive hardware required.

Serial Transmission

→

Data is transmitted bit by bit sequentially.

Example: USB and RS-232 use Serial Transmission.

Advantage: Reduces cost of transmission over parallel by roughly a factor of n.

Disadvantage: Slower than parallel transmission as bits are sent one at a time.

Parallel Transmission

→
→
→

Multiple bits transmitted simultaneously.

Example: Older computer buses, PCI, Parallel ATA.

Advantage:

  • -Speed.
  • -Can increase transfer speed by a factor of n over serial transmission.

Disadvantage:

  • -Cost.
  • -Limited to short distances due to signal degradation.
